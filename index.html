W.E.B Du Bois

William Edward Burghardt Du Bois (February 23, 1868 - August 27, 1963 age 95)

W.E.B. Du Bois has been an inspiration to me for a multitude of reasons. Du Bois has been a poineer for the African American population in the United States. He has written numerous books, helped establish the NAACP which has played a major role in the advancement of African Americans, and played a significant part in making America what it is today for the Black commnunity.

Books and Literature

  • The Souls of Black Folk
  • The Negro
  • Africa, Its Geography, People and Products
  • The Philadelphia Negro: A Social Study
  • Black Reconstruction in America

Notable Achievements

  • First African American to earn a Ph.D. from Harvard University
  • Co-Founder of the N.A.A.C.P.
  • Authored over 20 books and numerous publications about the African American experince in America and abroad.

Education

  • 1888 - Bachelors - Fisk University
  • 1890 - Bachelors - Harvard College (University) (cum laude)
  • 1892 - University of Berlin
  • 1895 - Ph. D - Harvard University (first African American Phd from HU)
